The Desgin Of The Georgia Font!

As a transitional serif design, Georgia shows a number of traditional features of serif typefaces from around the early 19th century, such as alternating thick and thin strokes, ball terminals, a vertical axis and an italic taking inspiration from calligraphy. Its figure designs are lower-case or text figures, designed to blend into continuous text, this was at the time a rare feature in computer fonts!
